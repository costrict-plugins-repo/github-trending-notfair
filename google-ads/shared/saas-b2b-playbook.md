# SaaS / B2B Product-Led Playbook

Use this archetype for SaaS, developer tools, MCP/tools, and product-led B2B acquisition accounts.

## Core posture

Cheap clicks are not success. Optimize toward activation and qualified product intent.

## Conversion hierarchy

Prefer primary conversions close to product value:

1. Paid purchase / qualified opportunity / connected account.
2. First successful product action or diagnosis.
3. First write/request/approval event when that indicates activation.
4. Signup/trial start only if activation is not yet available.
5. Button clicks/page views as secondary diagnostics only.

Do not switch to conversion-based bidding until tracking is reliable and there is enough signal.

## Query intent

High-quality:

- product category + exact workflow
- named integration + problem
- competitor/integration alternatives with clear landing pages
- bottom-funnel problem terms

Risky:

- generic AI/marketing/productivity curiosity
- "free no signup"
- tutorials/courses/definitions
- directories/marketplaces/lists when the product is not a directory
- terms implying affiliation with a platform when the brand relationship is unclear

## Brand trust and Limited Ad Serving

For new advertisers or tool/integration products:

- Make the advertiser brand explicit in headline 1 or above the fold.
- State the relationship clearly: independent tool/integration, not affiliated with platform owners.
- Mirror query intent on landing pages without implying ownership of third-party brands.
- Add trust elements: company identity, privacy/terms/contact, OAuth/data-access explanation, approval boundaries.
- Do not scale spend while Limited Ad Serving, weak QS, or zero activation signal remains unresolved.

## Budget posture

- Use Maximize Clicks only as a short discovery phase with search-term cleanup.
- Move toward Maximize Conversions when activation tracking is reliable.
- Add Target CPA only after observed activation/CAC economics are stable.
- Raise budget only after query quality and activation signal are proven.

## Anti-patterns

- Treating signup volume as quality when activation is absent.
- Buying broad generic AI/software terms before exact problem-aware terms are covered.
- Referencing third-party platforms in a way that creates unclear brand relationship risk.
- Scaling spend because CPC is low.
